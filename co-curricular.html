Impetus and Concepts

Impetus and Concepts is a technical event organized by PICT. This technical fest has eight events namely-Concepts, Pradnya, Pratibha, Naipunya, Tantra, Lakshya,Daksha and Yukti which will be held on 22nd, 23rd and 24th of March 2019. InC is an intercollegiate national level competition which has been catching the attention of corporate giants for sponsorship as well as guidance to budding entrepreneurs. Every year InC sets a platform for students to design, exhibit and watch their ideas come true. About 2400+ odd students participated from 150+ engineering colleges throughout India last year and we're confident to see even more this year.









ROBOCON

About PICT Robocon Team:

PICT has been participating in ABU Robocon since past 8 years. We stood 4th in India in 2014 and 11th in India in 2017.
 Students from all classes and departments are welcomed here. Robocon is a place where students can indulge their curiosity and the passion for robotics and dare to go beyond the boundaries of your academics to explore real life applications of their knowledge, learn new technologies and be exposed to national level competitions. The students also get to interact with over 90 colleges from all over the country, learn about designs, ideas and methods.

 

Technologies that we use and have used:

  • Pneumatic systems for actuation.
  • Automated Systems for robots.
  • Brushless DC motors.
  • High power Motor Drivers and reliable sensors for autonomous operation.
  • Servo motors for precise movements.
  • ATMEGA2560 micro-controller.
  • Arduino Uno, Mega etc micro-controllers.
  • Compass Module for navigation.
  • Electronic Duct Fans (EDFs) for thrust and indirect energy transfer.
  • Pixy for object detection based on colour.
  • PID Based control system.
  • Kinect for Digital Image Processing, Depth Sensing and Object tracking and Path detection and prediction of moving objects.
  • Multiple servo based mechanisms for gripping and moving static objects.

 

Robocon is an annual event which requires preparation,work and testing to be carried out for atleast 10 months a year. The team holds its introductory seminars and selection process for the interested third, second and first year students in the months of July and August-September respectively.

In 2014, the theme was A salute to parenthood. We were challenged to make robots which performed tasks of playing see-saw, swing and jungle gym. One of the robots, which was also called as parent robot, had to be manually controlled while the other one, called as a child robot, had to work autonomously.

In 2015, the theme was Robominton. The theme demanded us to build robots that could play a doubles game of badminton on a real sized court. The robots could either manually controlled or autonomously working.

In 2016, the theme was Clean Energy Recharging the world. The theme is to move a robot(eco-bot) through indirect energy transfer using another robot (Hybrid bot) through a given path, pick up a component from the eco-bot at the end of the path, climb the pole using the hybrid bot and place the picked up component at the top of the pole completing a windmill.

In 2017, the theme was Asobi: The Landing Disc. The theme resolves around the world asobi (play), which is also a fundamental philosophy behind robocon. In asobi, playful, unique, original show of skills is often more important than winning or losing, as everyone friend and foe alike can applaud and enjoy them. So, in the asobi spirit: we encorage playful, unique, original robot designs and strategies!

Tentative Dates of Robocon for every academic year.

Third year seminar and selection process : Month of July

Release of Robocon Theme and Tasks: Month end of August

Second and First year introductory seminar and selection process : In the months of August & September.

PICT IEEE Student Branch

About IEEE

IEEE, abbreviation of Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers is the worlds largest professional association dedicated to advancing technological innovation and excellence for the benefit of humanity. IEEE and its members are highly regarded for their publications and conferences in professional and educational activities.

The following information provides an overview of IEEE offerings and services.

IEEE Quick Facts

IEEE has:

  • 2,669 student branches at colleges and universities in 80 countries;
  • 334 Sections in ten geographic Regions worldwide;
  • more than 426,000 members in more than 160 countries, more than 50 percent of whom are from outside the United States;
  • more than 117,000 Student members;
  • 2,116 Chapters that unite local members with similar technical interests;
  • 922 student branch chapters of IEEE technical societies;
  • 440 affinity groups;

IEEE Affinity Groups are non-technical sub-units of one or more Sections or a Council. The Affinity Group patent entities are the IEEE-USA Consultants Network, Young Professionals (YP), Women in Engineering (WIE), and Life Members (LM).

Credenz

(http://www.credenz.info)

PICT IEEE Student Branch (PISB), one of the most active student branches of IEEE, is a proud host of the annual technical event Credenz. It is one of the most innovative scientific technical and management events held in August each year. It is organized exclusively by students of PISB. It has grown manifold since its inception in 2004, and has made a name for itself as a technical renaissance showcasing the young emerging minds of technocrats and administrators. Through its deluge of competitions, lectures, exhibitions and workshops Credenz endeavours to enkindle the minds of thousands of students visiting annually. Over the years, it has attracted the connoisseurs of engineering and technology.

Credenz represents zenith of science and engineering in a landscape of future visionaries and scientists. From thrilling competitions to the enriching workshops, every bit promises to be a fulfilling experience. Workshops conducted by experts for the budding engineers, seminars conducted by aficionados on upcoming technologies were focal points of the event. The event has been a symposium of young minds from numerous colleges across the nation to participate in innovative events and inculcate new technicalities.

We, members of PISB, carry with us an unrelinquishable fire and an indomitable determination to make CREDENZ a world-class event.

 

Credenz Tech Dayz (CTD)

(ctd.credenz.info)

Credenz Tech Dayz(CTD) is organized by the PICT IEEE Student Branch annually, in the even semester, to coincide with the IEEE membership drive. CTD brings with it a gusto that is portrayed in the form of seminars by esteemed industrialists. These seminars provide a platform wherein the students are exposed to the latest innovations in technology. They also get an opportunity to interact with experts from the industry, thus giving them further insights into and helping them to develop their skills in the technical field.

Many seminars were organized as a part of CTD15. Experts from companies like IDEA Cellular, Airtight Networks, Tech Mahindra shared their knowledge with us. The seminars on Robotics, Android App Studio and Gaming and Related Technologies drew deep interest from the students, while those on Wifi and IoT and Telecommunication unfolding were also immensely appreciated. The seminars on areas like 3D-printing and Big Data shed light on concepts heard by all but known to very few. A seminar on start-up was useful for learning more about how a business is initiated and works.

PICT ACM Student Chapter

 

PICT ACM Student Chapter(PASC) was started in March 2011 with a vision to carry on ACM Internationals intentions on a grass root level in PICT. PASC was founded with initial team of 4 members and as of now it is PASC is kept active by nearly 220 ACM members. We aim to provide opportunities to students by organizing world-class seminars, lectures, and workshops to meet peers and experts in their fields of interest. Every year an event known as PULZION is organized by members of PASC to offer platform for students to showcase their talents in technical and non-technical fields.

Our mission is to offer students an opportunity to learn, innovate and growinto intellectuals, visionaries, and technocrats, who work to promote the advancement of science and technology.

Every year we impart knowledge to hundreds of students and help them understand how they can contribute to the field of computing science. We conduct several technical events in a year. We nurture the growth of inquisitive minds by providing them with the toolkit to excel in various fields.

The chapter organizes seminars and workshops on different domains by speakers who are well-known in their fields, which give students opportunities to learn about advanced research subjects. The chapter activities include events, seminars, competitions, academia-industry initiatives and various ventures to promote knowledge sharing and innovation.

PICT CSI Student Branch

Pune Institute of Computer Technology (PICT) CSI Student Branch was established in 2016 with an objective to facilitate research, knowledge sharing, learning and career enhancement for the students, while simultaneously inspiring and nurturing new entrants into the industry and helping them to integrate into the IT Community.

PCSB works under Computer Society of India (CSI) to provide latest technology related knowledge to students. PCSB started with 147 student members in academic year 2016-2017.

PCSB arranges events like Codeventure, Blind coding and different types of workshops (conducted by industry experts) throughout the year.. PCSB also arranges Enthusia every year.

PICT IET Student Chapter

Since PISCs inception in 2012, the PICT IET Student Chapter (PISC) has always aimed at preparing students and providing them with that extra edge for success in the professional world they step into. The chapter offers a plethora of opportunities to its student body to hone their technical skills and with a speedy growth, PISC has now gone on to become the largest student chapter in the state.

                      PICT IET Student Chapter have been actively involved in organising various activities ,events to cater technical nourishment of its memers. Also PISC has been in the forefront to organize different events & seminars for engineering students in Pune. PISC conducts the technical event Scientia annually.

Epictomize:

(IET Magazine)            

PISC launched its first annual technical magazine ePICTomize during Scientia 14. The theme for the first issue was SPEED. The magazine contained articles on various topics ranging from science, technology and entertainment to sports and video games. Most of the articles were submitted by students with a special article from Volkswagen India. The magazine also contained an interview of Mr Shripad Yawalkar, Chemical Weapons Inspector, OPCW. It also contained a section based on the activities conducted by PISC. The highlight of the magazine was that, keeping in mind the wastage of paper that occurs in printing, we launched the e-copy of the magazine on various platforms. ePICTomize 15 with the theme of Dimensions was launched on 12th of September during inauguration of our flagship event Scientia 15.
